"Embracing the Benefits of AI While Avoiding Over-Reliance: Finding a Balance for Creative Advancement"

“Balancing AI’s Benefits with Human Creativity”

photo iStock.com

With the advent of artificial intelligence, it seems almost superfluous to devote oneself to honing a skill and refining the fundamental aspects that contribute to one’s character. While this development is not inherently nefarious, it brings about an unsettling feeling of detachment as AI becomes more integrated with humanity.

As AI becomes increasingly proficient in serving our daily routines, we may gradually become less reliant on our own faculties and more artificial in our behaviour. A mediocre writer could potentially become a great one, with the assistance of AI algorithms that facilitate a unique writing style, replete with an individualized vocabulary and compelling plotline. This begs the question: what kind of storytelling will AI eventually produce?

As we become more capable of expressing ourselves through AI, we must consider whether this will enhance our creative abilities or represent a negative development. Would it truly be a creative transgression if an AI program were to write the script for the next blockbuster film, with the writer merely providing a plot and allowing the program to generate a polished product? Or would it simply represent a new creative trademark?

The key to maximizing the benefits of AI lies in learning to manipulate it to one’s creative advantage. In the field of programming, for instance, AI systems can be used as instantaneous coding machines that save time, reduce errors, and bring value to human counterparts. While learning to program can be a positive and rewarding experience, it would be a disservice not to take advantage of AI as a tool to expedite coding and undertake otherwise daunting projects.

If we rely too heavily on artificial intelligence to supplement our existence without counteracting its potential to dominate our lives, we risk regressing into brain-dead humanoids, akin to phytoplankton. While this might not be so terrible (after all, we rely on plankton for survival), it’s important to acknowledge the limitations of AI and not become overly dependent on it.
